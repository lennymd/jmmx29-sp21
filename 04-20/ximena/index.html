Love may be in the air, but

Over recent decades. the universality of marriage has been on a decline. The proportion of women remaining single in their 30s and 40s has climbed exponentially, especially in the large cities and among the better educated.

East Asian Family Dynamics

East Asian families place high emphasis in ‘strong’ family ties. It is common for married couples to live together with their in-laws and children all under one roof. One particularly distinctive feature of East Asian families is a clearly-defined gender division of labor within the family.

The patriarchal family system has put women at social and economic disadvantages. Many traditional marriages are characterized by the lack of independence.

East Asia & Pacific

Two forces are giving women more autonomy: education and jobs. Women’s education in East Asia has impacted dramatically over the past 30 years. In South Korea now, women earn half of all master’s degrees. Education increases job prospects for women. Therefore allowing them to be earn more so they don’t need to solely rely on their significant other. However, in Japan, a strong tradition of women being housewives after marriage still persists. One of the reasons why women are now opting out of marriage is to pursue a career in what they love. In sum, Freedom and independence are the two factors that explains the declining rate and delayed marriage in East Asia.
