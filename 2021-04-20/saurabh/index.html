Criminalization of Immigrants

Before the new anti-immigration laws were enacted, the number of undocumented immigrants were less as the majority of undocumented immigrants from Mexico used to travel between the US and Mexico. With the stringent crackdown initiated by the current administration, the number of undocumented immigrants entering US increased as they preferred to stay out in the US rather than risk crossing the US- Mexico border out of fear of being prosecuted and eventual deportation, as mandated by the new anti-immigration laws.

IRCA: The Immigration Reform and Control Act

Made it illegal to hire illegal immigrants knowingly and establishing financial and other penalties for companies that employed illegal immigrants.

Anti-Drug Abuse Act

Created the list of offenses which will count as aggravated felony charges and non-citizens were eligible for removal or deportation if they commit an aggravated felony.

Immigration Act

Expanded the list of offenses which will count as aggravated felony charges and non-citizens were eligible for removal or deportation if they commit an aggravated felony.

AEDPA: Anti-terrorism and Effective Death Penalty Act

Required the mandatory detention of non-citizens convicted of a wide range of offenses including minor drug offenses.

IRAIRA: Illegal Immigration Reform and Immigrant Responsibility Act

Expanded the list of offenses including unlawful presence in US which required mandatory detention of non-citizens.

Incarceration of Immigrants

The stringent immigration crackdown initiated by the various administrations have resulted in a tremendous increase in the number of immigrants that have been apprehended by the US Border patrol at the US- Mexico border. The current administration has emphasized that all immigrants crossing the border must be separated from the family, even minor children and sent to the detention centers located all over the country in order to ease the burden at the border detention facilities. Typically, many undocumented immigrants cross over the border as families along with single adults and minor unaccompanied children making up the numbers over the years. The administration believes that separating the families including minor children and shipping them across different detention centers all over the country will serve as a strong deterrence. Thus, detention centers all over the country are housing immigrants detained at the southwest border

Profit & Abuses

The various administrations have prioritized the capture and eventual deportation of the undocumented immigrants. This initiative has created the need for increased prisons and detention centers to process and hold the undocumented immigrants until they are eventually deported. Thus, the authorities have started awarding contracts to private, for-profit providers of prison services to house more immigrants. Needless to say, the detention of undocumented immigrants has become a profitable business and there are fears that many for- profit contractors are increasingly bidding to establish such prisons to meet the demands of the present administration.
