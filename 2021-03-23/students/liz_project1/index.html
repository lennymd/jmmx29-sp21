I sit on my mat in easy pose, right shin crossed in front of the left. Move the flesh from under my sit bones and connect to the earth, pelvis neutral, hands on my knees, and elongate the spine. Shrug my shoulders back and down away from my ears, lift and open my chest, and close my eyes. These are just a few of the adjustments that I make as I settle in for my practice. Breathing through my nose, I breath in—four, three, two, one—I breath out—four, three, two, one, and repeat for a few cycles of breath. Hands to my heart in prayer position, and set the intention for my practice. The day melts away. My mind quiet. My thoughts still. I feel ease.

Poses to Calm Your Mind and Promote Ease

A variety of poses are available to promote well-being. Beginner level poses such as corpse pose, child’s pose and legs-up-the-wall pose are beneficial for all who practice and accessible to those just starting out. And there are intermediate level poses for more seasoned yogis. The Sanskrit name of each pose has been included.

Pose Benefits at a Glance

These yoga poses provide a variety of health benefits to promote ease of the mind and alleviate physical distress that may result from mental and emotional dis-“ease.”

Trends

A 2019 survey of 1,200 yoga practitioners reveals more than half practice yoga to destress and generally feel better.
